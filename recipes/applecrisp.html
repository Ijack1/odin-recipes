What's our secret to our best Apple Crisp recipe?

Apple crisp is the perfect dessert for when you want the warm and comforting flavors of an apple pie but don't want to put in the effort to make one. Often confused with a crumble, this easy-to-make deep-dish dessert has apple filling with a nubby, streusel-like topping. And typically, an apple crisp has oats in the topping that adds a little crunch.

To inspire you to get baking, we've rounded up the Best Apple Crisp recipe of all time. This recipe include traditional apple crisp with oatmeal as well as new takes like caramel apple crisp and apple and pomegranate crisp. How to make our best Apple Crisp: get the full recipe below

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups apples - peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1 cup sifted all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup butter, softened

Method

Step 1

Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Lightly grease an 8-inch square baking dish.

Step 2

Arrange apple slices evenly in the prepared baking dish.

Step 3

Sift flour, sugar, cinnamon, and salt into a bowl.

Step 4

Cut in butter using a pastry blender, or 2 knives, until mixture resembles coarse cornmeal; sprinkle over apples.
